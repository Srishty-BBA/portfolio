01
Major Research Project · BBA 6th Sem · IGDTUW · Guided by Dr. Ankush Goyal
CSR Expenditure & Financial Performance — Evidence from the Indian IT Sector
Panel data study (FY2019–25) across 11 Indian IT firms using OLS regression. Tested whether mandatory CSR under Section 135 of the Companies Act, 2013 reflects genuine corporate conscience or mechanically calibrated compliance. Applied Slack Resources Theory (Bourgeois, 1981) and Institutional Theory (DiMaggio & Powell, 1983) to India's first-of-its-kind mandatory CSR framework.

FY19–25

The verdict: In the Indian IT sector, CSR expenditure is primarily a mathematical function of Net Profit After Tax — not voluntary conscience. The PAT coefficient of 0.023 is statistically indistinguishable from the 2% statutory threshold. TCS maintains its CSR-to-PAT ratio in a tight 1.72%–2.19% band across 5 years. Only the unexplained ~20% of variance is where genuine corporate intent lives — and that is where analysts should focus.
